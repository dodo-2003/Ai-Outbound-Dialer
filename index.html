Case Study — Voice AI

An AI dialer that makes a five-person team’s calls before lunch.

A US medical billing company’s cold-calling team was capped by human hours — five callers, ~2,250 conversations a month. I designed and built the AI system that does the same qualification work: a voice agent, a compliant dialing backbone, and the dashboard that proves every call.

The Problem

The client’s five-person team made 43,220 calls over three months — a 16% answer rate, 51 seconds average duration. Every extra conversation meant another salary, and quality varied caller to caller. Front-desk gatekeeping and accent friction made it worse.

The ask: automate outbound qualification calls to US healthcare providers — without becoming a compliance problem, and without losing visibility into what the AI actually says on the phone.

The System
System architecture — lead upload to verified, costed call
01

The voice agent

Built on Vapi with Deepgram Flux for speech-to-text, Claude for reasoning and ElevenLabs for voice — with a full system prompt covering qualification flow, objection handling, voicemail detection, and structured JSON output for every call.
02

The dialing backbone

n8n workflows with atomic lead-claiming so no lead is ever called twice, a full state machine (raw → queued → calling → qualified / DNC), and idempotent end-of-call webhooks that survive retries without duplicating data.
03

The ops dashboard

Flask + PostgreSQL 16: KPIs, every call’s transcript and recording, itemized per-call cost, xlsx lead upload and a one-click campaign start. Deployed with Docker Compose behind a Cloudflare Tunnel.
04

The lead supply

A scheduled n8n pipeline ingests the public NPI registry weekly — downloads, filters organizational providers, builds a fresh spreadsheet and emails it to the team. The dialer never runs dry.

    Engineering Notes
    01

    Compliance by design

    TCPA calling hours enforced per US timezone before any dial; do-not-call exclusion at the queue level, not the agent level — a lead on the DNC list can never reach the phone layer.
    02

    Latency tuning

    Voice response latency profiled and tuned from ~1,400 ms toward a 600–700 ms target — the difference between a caller that feels robotic and one people actually talk to.
    03

    Cost transparency

    Every call’s cost is itemized in the dashboard — speech-to-text, LLM, voice, telephony — so the client sees exactly what each conversation costs, not a monthly surprise.
